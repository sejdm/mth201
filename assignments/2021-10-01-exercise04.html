  1. Show that the curvature at any point of a line segment is always 0.

  2. Find a parametrization of an ellipse, i.e. \(\{(x,y) \in \mathbb{R}^2 \ |\ \frac{x^2}{a} + \frac{y^2}{b} = 1\}\) and use it compute its curvature function \(\kappa(t)\).

  3. Given any smooth parametrization, \(\gamma : (\alpha, \beta) \to \mathbb{R}^2\), is the curvature function \(\kappa(t)\) always smooth? Do you need to add some condition? What is it?
