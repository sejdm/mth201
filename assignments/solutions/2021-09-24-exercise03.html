Hints / Solutions to Exercise sheet 3

Last modified: 08:30 PM - 17 October, 2021

PDF version

Question 1: In an earlier exercise you found the parametrization of a line segment joining two points. Use that parametrization to find the arc length of the line segment in terms of its end points. Try with some other parametrization too.

Solution 1: The line segment between \(p\) and \(q\) can be obtained by considering the displacement vector \(q-p\). The parametrization should be so that at \(t=0\), \(p\) is not being displaced at all, but when \(t=1\), \(p\) is displaced to \(q\):

\[\gamma(t) = p + t(q-p)\] \[\dot{\gamma}(t) = (q-p)\] \[\|\dot{\gamma}(t)\| = \|q-p\|\] \[\int_0^1\|\dot{\gamma}(t)\mathrm{d}t\| = \int_0^1\|q-p\|\mathrm{d}t = \|q-p\|\]

Question 2: These steps will show that the line segment joining two points is the shortest possible curve joining the two points:

Solution 2: Remember that \(\mathbf{v} . \mathbf{w} = \|\mathbf{v}\|\|\mathbf{w}\|\cos(\theta)\) where \(\theta\) is the angle between \(\mathbf{v}\) and \(\mathbf{w}\). Part 1.. easily follows from \(\cos(\theta)\leq 1\) for any angle \(\theta\).

For part 2, \[\mathbf{v}.\frac{\mathbf{v}}{\|\mathbf{v}\|}= \frac{1}{\|\mathbf{v}\|}(\mathbf{v}.\mathbf{v}) = \frac{\|\mathbf{v}\|^2}{\| \mathbf{v}\|} = \|\mathbf{v}\|\] This part allows you to express the norm in a way that will allow you to use the fundamental theorem of calculus in the next part.
