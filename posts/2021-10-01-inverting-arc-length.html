The arc-length function is defined,

\[s(t) := \int_{t_0}^t\|\dot{\gamma}(u)\|\mathrm{d}u\]

We used the inverse of \(s(t)\) to reparametrize to a unit speed parametrization. However, we can only do that if it is indeed invertible and the inverse is smooth. That \(s(t)\) is smooth was already demonstrated in the lecture.

To prove that is invertible, we need to check if it is one-one, i.e. if \(x\) and \(y\) are different, then so is \(s(x)\) and \(s(y)\).
